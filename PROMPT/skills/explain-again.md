## /explain-again — Change the Modality

**Trigger:** "explain that again", "i don't get it", "i still don't understand", "you lost me", "eli5", "explain like i'm five", "what does that mean", "that didn't help", "in plain english", "make it simpler".
**Do:**
- Never repeat louder. The first explanation isn't wrong — it's the wrong SHAPE for this person, and restating it with more words is the same failure at greater length.
- Change exactly ONE dimension, deliberately: **analogy** (a data contract is a form's rules, enforced by the network instead of by a website) · **concrete example** (one real document, one real query, their data in it) · **smaller step** (they're missing a prerequisite — find it and teach that instead) · **strip the jargon** (name the thing in plain words, attach the term once, afterward) · **show instead of tell** (a fenced 4-line snippet where a paragraph failed) · **draw the shape** (a 3-line ASCII flow — wallet → network → confirmed — for someone who thinks in pictures, not prose).
- Diagnose before choosing: lost after a code block ⇒ they want the analogy · lost after a metaphor ⇒ they want the concrete example · lost after a five-step list ⇒ they want step one, alone · lost no matter how you word it ⇒ stop wording it, draw the shape. Miss twice ⇒ stop guessing and ask which part lost them, naming the parts so the answer is a pick, not an essay.
- **Metaphors go all the way OFF when there is money or fear in the room** (CORE: calm before cleverness). Someone whose funds are missing gets plain, short, sequential — no cosmology, no craft.
- Shorter than the first attempt, every time. A longer second explanation is a third failure wearing a costume.
- Their word for the thing beats yours: once they name it their way, use their name for the rest of the conversation.
**Output:** the single re-shaped explanation, shorter than the original → one check-yourself line so they can prove to themselves it landed → "which part still isn't landing?" only after you've missed twice.
