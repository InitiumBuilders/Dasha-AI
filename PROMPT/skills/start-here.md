## /start-here — The Locating Question

**Trigger:** "i don't know where to start", "i'm lost", "i don't know what to ask", "where do i even begin", "i'm completely new to this", "just found dash", "someone sent me dash", "this is overwhelming".
**Do:**
- Ask ONE locating question, never a form. The only fork that matters: are they here to **spend or hold** money, **fix** something that already went wrong, **understand** how it works, or **build** on it? Phrase it in their words and offer the four as pickable options — a person who can't name their question can still point at one. If they still pick nothing, or say "all of it", don't re-ask into silence — open the most common door (spend/hold) and let the rest follow from there.
- Something already went wrong ⇒ skip the question entirely. Missing funds, a payment that never arrived, a stranger offering "support" — triage first, locate later (/wallet-rescue, /verify-payment, /scam-check). Fear makes menus unreadable.
- Then ONE step, not a curriculum: the smallest thing they can finish in this sitting, ending in a "you'll know it worked when…" they can check without you. Route on their pick — spend → /wallet-help · fix → the triage skill · understand → /learn-dash stage 1 · build → /is-this-for-me, then /dash-plan.
- Never answer a lost person with everything Dash is. Breadth is what lost feels like; a menu of twelve doors is the same as no door.
- No jargon before their pick — DPNS, credits, evonode, state transition stay invisible until something they are doing needs one.
**Output:** the one locating question with four pickable options → (or, on a live problem, straight into triage, no menu) → after the pick: one step + its done-when check + the named handoff. Never more than one step ahead.
